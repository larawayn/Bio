Background

Noah Laraway is a current graduate student at Syracuse University pursuing a Masters of Science in Applied Data Science. His expected graduation date is September 2022.

Before that he worked in the oil and gas industry as an engineer and in technical sales for over 14 years. As an engineer he worked as the sole technical expert on oil sites overseeing hydraulic fracturing operations. He was involved in various projects including optimizing fracturing designs and fracturing fluids as well as mentoring new engineers.

In technical sales he was responsible for managing over thirty accounts that brought in upwards of $400 Million in revenue annually. In addition he performed numerous technical sales presentations focusing on implementing new technologies to drive efficency and increase oil well production.

In 2020 Noah decided it was time to move on from oil and gas. Due to the explosive growth in the data industry and his technical background, he decided to enroll in University of Denver's Data Analytics Boot Camp program. He soon learned that working with data was something he found extremely enjoyable and rewarding. Shortly after that Noah came to the conclusion that he wanted to further pursue working in the data industry and start the Masters program at Syracuse University.
